# OptiFlow OS Code of Conduct

**Version 1.0 — Effective upon adoption**

---

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and maintainers of the OptiFlow OS community, pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age, body size, disability (visible or invisible)
- Ethnicity, caste, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression
- Level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality
- Personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation
- Language, dialect, or communication style

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community — one that reflects the **Indian MSMEs** we serve and the global open-source ecosystem we are part of.

---

## Why This Code of Conduct Exists

OptiFlow OS is an open-core platform built for **Indian MSMEs** — a community that spans manufacturing units, logistics companies, retail chains, service businesses, and the developers who build tools for them. Our contributors include:

- **Frontend engineers** working in Vue 3 and TypeScript
- **Backend engineers** building Frappe/Django APIs
- **Designers** improving the Doer, Captain, and Admin panels
- **Documentation writers** maintaining the product gallery and architecture docs
- **Translators** helping with English, Hindi, and Hinglish locales
- **MSME operators** providing real-world feedback
- **Enterprise customers** deploying at scale

This Code of Conduct ensures that all these voices can collaborate productively and respectfully.

## Expected Behavior

### In All Interactions

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accept constructive criticism
- Focus on what is best for the community and the product's users (Indian MSMEs)
- Show empathy towards other community members
- Assume good faith — most contributors are trying to help

### In Code Reviews

- Review the code, not the person. Critique implementation choices, not the author.
- Explain your reasoning. "This won't work because..." is better than "This is wrong."
- Offer alternatives. If you reject an approach, suggest what would work.
- Be patient with newer contributors. Everyone started somewhere.
- Acknowledge good solutions — not just problems.

### In Issue Discussions

- Stay on topic. Keep the discussion focused on the issue at hand.
- Provide evidence: screenshots, logs, reproduction steps, references.
- If you disagree, state your case with reasoning, not emotion.
- If you're blocked, ask for help clearly and politely.
- If you're helping, be specific and actionable.

### In Documentation

- Write clearly and concisely. Not everyone is a native English speaker.
- Use examples. Show, don't just tell.
- Keep screenshots up to date. Outdated visuals confuse more than they help.
- Respect the product name: **OptiFlow OS** — always capitalized, always with "OS".
- Respect the brand: use assets from `src/assets/branding/`, never hardcode paths.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable within our community:

### Harassment & Discrimination

- Violent threats or language directed against another person
- Discriminatory jokes and language based on the protected characteristics listed in [Our Pledge](#our-pledge)
- Personal insults, especially those using racist, sexist, casteist, homophobic, transphobic, or ableist terms
- Sexual language, imagery, or unwelcome advances of any kind
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following (online or in-person)
- Publishing others' private information without explicit consent (doxxing)
- Sustained disruption of community discussions, meetings, or events

### Conduct-Specific Prohibitions

- **Trolling**: Posting inflammatory content with the intent to provoke
- **Spam**: Unsolicited promotion, advertising, or repetitive off-topic content
- **Impersonation**: Pretending to be a project maintainer, OptiFlow Technologies employee, or another contributor
- **Brand misuse**: Using "OptiFlow OS" or the OptiFlow logo in a way that implies endorsement, affiliation, or official status without authorization
- **License violations**: Encouraging others to violate the AGPL v3 or Commercial License terms
- **Malicious contributions**: Submitting code with intentional vulnerabilities, backdoors, or data exfiltration
- **False reporting**: Knowingly filing false Code of Conduct or security reports

### Communication-Specific Prohibitions

- Using GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, or Discussions as a platform for unrelated political, religious, or social advocacy
- Aggressive or demanding tone in feature requests or bug reports
- Repeatedly asking the same question after it has been answered
- Bypassing project processes (e.g., emailing maintainers directly for support rather than using Issues)

## Communication Guidelines

### GitHub Issues

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Search existing issues before filing a new one | File duplicates without checking first |
| Use the issue templates (Bug Report, Feature Request) | Post vague one-liners |
| Include clear reproduction steps, environment, and screenshots | Assume maintainers can reproduce without context |
| Be patient — maintainers are often volunteers or working on multiple priorities | Bump issues repeatedly ("any updates?" in short intervals) |
| Engage constructively in the discussion | Derail the thread with off-topic commentary |

### Pull Requests

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Follow the PR template and checklist | Submit PRs without running local checks |
| Keep changes focused (one feature/fix per PR) | Combine unrelated changes in one PR |
| Respond to review feedback promptly | Ignore reviewer comments |
| Update documentation and screenshots when relevant | Leave documentation stale |
| Ask for clarification if a review comment is unclear | Argue defensively without understanding the concern |

### Code Reviews

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Review with a collaborative mindset | Review with an adversarial mindset |
| Explain the "why" behind your suggestions | Give commands without context ("Change this.") |
| Acknowledge good code, not just problems | Only comment on what's wrong |
| Separate "required changes" from "nitpicks" clearly | Mix blocking issues with style preferences |
| Be timely — review within 2 business days when possible | Leave PRs unreviewed for weeks |

### Community Discussions

- Use GitHub Discussions for: feature brainstorming, architectural RFCs, Q&A, showcase, and meta-discussions
- Search before posting to avoid duplicates
- Keep discussions respectful and evidence-based
- Use appropriate categories and labels

## Code Review Conduct

Code review is one of the most important collaborative activities in open source. We hold code reviews to a high standard of professionalism.

### For Reviewers

1. **Review the code, not the person.** Avoid language like "you made a mistake" — instead say "this approach has a limitation."
2. **Be specific.** "This doesn't handle the offline case" is actionable. "This is wrong" is not.
3. **Distinguish blocking from non-blocking.** Label critical issues clearly; use "nit" for style preferences.
4. **Consider the contributor's experience level.** For first-time contributors, provide more context and guidance.
5. **Respond in a reasonable timeframe.** If you can't review fully, acknowledge the PR and set expectations.
6. **Praise good work.** Positive reinforcement encourages more contributions.

### For Contributors

1. **Assume good faith.** Reviewers are trying to make the project better.
2. **Ask for clarification.** If a comment is unclear, say so politely.
3. **Push back with evidence.** If you disagree with a review, explain your reasoning with technical justification.
4. **Iterate.** Be willing to make changes. Multiple rounds of review are normal.
5. **Don't take it personally.** Code review is about the code, not about you.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers have additional responsibilities to the community:

### Fair Treatment

- Apply the Code of Conduct consistently to all contributors, regardless of their status, affiliation, or contribution volume
- Do not show favoritism to enterprise customers, paying users, or personal acquaintances
- Enforce rules equally for first-time contributors and core team members

### Transparency

- Communicate moderation decisions clearly (within privacy constraints)
- Document governance decisions in project repositories
- Explain the reasoning behind rejection of features, PRs, or proposals

### Neutral Moderation

- Do not use moderator authority to win technical disagreements
- Recuse yourself from moderation decisions involving personal conflicts
- When uncertain, escalate to another maintainer

### Enforcement Consistency

- Enforce the Code of Conduct based on observable behavior, not assumptions
- Apply escalating consequences consistently (see [Enforcement Policy](#enforcement-policy))
- Document enforcement actions confidentially for future reference

### Contributor Support

- Welcome and mentor new contributors
- Provide constructive feedback on contributions
- Help contributors navigate the project's processes and standards
- Acknowledge and appreciate contributions publicly

## Enforcement Policy

### Severity Levels

| Level | Description | Example | Consequence |
|-------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **1 — Warning** | Unintentional minor violation | First instance of harsh language, off-topic comment, or minor insensitivity | Private warning + request to modify behavior |
| **2 — Temporary Restriction** | Repeated or intentional minor violations | Continuing disruptive behavior after a warning | 7-day restriction from participating in Issues/PRs |
| **3 — Temporary Suspension** | Serious violation or pattern of harmful behavior | Harassment, personal attacks, sustained trolling | 30-day suspension from all community spaces |
| **4 — Permanent Ban** | Extreme violation or failure to reform | Hate speech, doxxing, threats, spam campaigns, repeated violations after suspension | Permanent removal from all community spaces |

### Enforcement Workflow

```
Report Received
      │
      ▼
  [72h] Acknowledgment to reporter
      │
      ▼
  [7 days] Investigation
      │
      ├── Insufficient evidence → Report closed, reporter notified
      │
      └── Violation confirmed → Severity assessment
               │
               ▼
          Enforcement action (Level 1–4)
               │
               ▼
          Respondent notified + given opportunity to respond
               │
               ▼
          Respondent accepts? → Action enforced
               │
               └── Respondent appeals? → Escalated to appeals panel
```

### Appeals

Anyone subjected to an enforcement action may appeal within **14 days** of notification:

1. Submit an appeal to appeals@optiflowos.com
2. Include: your name, the enforcement action, the context, and your reasoning
3. Appeals are reviewed by a panel of at least two maintainers not involved in the original decision
4. The panel's decision is final

### Confidentiality

- All reports are kept confidential to the extent possible
- Reporter identity is only shared with those who need to know for the investigation
- Enforcement actions are communicated privately unless public disclosure is necessary (e.g., banning a spam account)
- Case details are never discussed publicly

## Reporting Process

### How to Report a Violation

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

| Channel | Contact | Best For |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Email** | conduct@optiflowos.com | All reports |
| **GitHub** | Private @mention a maintainer | Minor concerns, questions |
| **In-person** | Directly to any maintainer | Live events, workshops |

### What to Include

- **Your contact**: Name and email (for follow-up)
- **Details**: Date, time, platform (GitHub issue/PR/discussion), link if applicable
- **Description**: What happened, who was involved, what was said or done
- **Context**: Any relevant background, prior interactions, or patterns
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, logs, or saved messages (do not edit or fabricate)
- **Desired outcome**: What would make you feel the situation is resolved

### Confidentiality

- All reports are treated as confidential
- Reporter identity is protected
- Reports are shared only with maintainers who need to know for the investigation
- You will not be retaliated against for reporting in good faith

### Response Timeline

| Step | Target |
|------|--------|
| Acknowledgment | Within 72 hours |
| Initial assessment | Within 7 days |
| Resolution | Within 30 days (most cases) |

## Diversity & Inclusion

### Our Commitment

OptiFlow OS serves a diverse user base — Indian MSMEs across manufacturing, logistics, retail, and services. Our contributor community must reflect that diversity.

We are committed to:

- **Equal opportunity**: All contributors are evaluated on their contributions, not their background
- **Global participation**: We welcome contributors from every country, culture, and time zone
- **Language respect**: While English is the primary language for code and issues, we respect contributors for whom English is not a first language. Be patient and clarifying, not dismissive.
- **Beginner friendliness**: We actively welcome first-time open-source contributors. Labeled issues (`good first issue`, `help wanted`) are gateways, not tests.

OptiFlow OS supports English, Hindi, and Hinglish in the application. Our community primarily communicates in English, but:

- Issues and discussions in Hindi or Hinglish are welcome where they help clarity

## Conflict Resolution

### Principles

1. **Address issues early** — small disagreements are easier to resolve than entrenched conflicts
2. **Focus on the problem, not the person** — technical disagreements are about code, architecture, and priorities
3. **Seek understanding first** — ask clarifying questions before forming judgments
4. **Use escalating support** — start with direct conversation, escalate to maintainers if needed

### Resolution Paths

| Conflict Type | First Step | If Unresolved | Final |
|--------------|------------|---------------|-------|
| Technical disagreement | Discuss in the PR/issue thread | Involve a maintainer for technical decision | Core Maintainer consensus |
| Behavioral concern | Private message to the person | Report to conduct@optiflowos.com | Code of Conduct enforcement |
| Governance question | GitHub Discussion | Discuss with Core Maintainers | Project Owner decision |
| Licensing / Legal | Email legal@optiflowos.com | — | OptiFlow Technologies legal |

### Community Restoration

After a conflict is resolved:

- Enforcement actions are not held against contributors long-term
- Contributors who have served suspensions are welcome to rejoin the community
- Apologies and amends are encouraged but not required
- The goal is to restore a productive working relationship, not to punish

## FAQ

### Why do we need a Code of Conduct?

A Code of Conduct sets clear expectations for behavior, protects community members from harassment, and provides a framework for resolving conflicts. It makes the community safer, more welcoming, and more productive.

### Who enforces it?

The Core Maintainers are responsible for enforcing the Code of Conduct. They may delegate enforcement of minor issues to other maintainers. The Project Owner has final authority on all enforcement decisions.

### How do I report issues?

Email **conduct@optiflowos.com** with details of the incident. All reports are kept confidential. See [Reporting Process](#reporting-process) for full instructions.

### Can I appeal a decision?

Yes. Submit an appeal to **appeals@optiflowos.com** within 14 days of the enforcement action. A panel of at least two maintainers not involved in the original decision will review it. See [Appeals](#appeals) for details.

### How are investigations handled?

Upon receiving a report, maintainers acknowledge receipt within 72 hours. They gather information from all parties involved, assess severity, and determine the appropriate enforcement action. Investigations are kept confidential, and all parties are treated with respect.

### How is confidentiality protected?

Reports are shared only with maintainers who need to know for the investigation. Reporter identity is never shared publicly. Case details are not discussed outside the investigating team. See [Confidentiality](#confidentiality) for more.

### What if I disagree with a technical decision?

Technical disagreements are normal and healthy. Discuss them in the relevant Issue or PR thread. If you cannot reach consensus, a maintainer will make a final decision. Maintainers should explain their reasoning, and you should accept the outcome gracefully.

### Can I use the OptiFlow logo in my own project?

You may use the OptiFlow logo to refer to the project (e.g., "integrated with OptiFlow OS"). You may not modify the logo or use it in a way that implies endorsement. See [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md) Part III for full terms.
